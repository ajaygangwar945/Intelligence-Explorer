Generative AI Foundations

Designing models that don't just see the world, but create it anew.

Generative AI represents a paradigm shift in computing—moving from Discriminative models (that recognize patterns) to Generative models (that create them).

"What I cannot create, I do not understand." - Richard Feynman

Core Generative Models

  • GANs (Generative Adversarial Networks) A high-stakes digital duel. A 'Generator' tries to create fake data, while a 'Discriminator' tries to spot the forgery. Both get better through competition.
  • VAEs (Variational Autoencoders) A structural approach. These models compress data into a small "latent" space and then attempt to reconstruct it perfectly from that core.
  • Diffusion Models The new standard. These models learn by taking an image, adding noise until it's unrecognizable, and then learning to reverse that process perfectly.

The Ethical Frontier

With great power comes great responsibility. Generative AI brings significant challenges:

  • Deepfakes: The risk of creating misleading or harmful synthetic media.
  • Bias: Models can inadvertently amplify stereotypes found in their training data.
  • Privacy: Ensuring that models don't "memorize" and reveal sensitive training data.
